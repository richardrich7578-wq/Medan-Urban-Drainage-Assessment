Medan City Urban Drainage Service Blind Spots and Flood Vulnerability Assessment

 Project Overview
This project visualizes the urban drainage network in Medan City(Indonesia)and identifies flood vulnerability hotspots. By mapping critical infrastructure(school&hospital)against the effective service area of existing waterways, this spatial analysis highlights regions lacking adequate drainage protection.

 Tools & Data
Software:" QGIS "
Data Sources:" Administrative Boundaries: GADM "
Waterways & Critical Facilities (Hospitals/Schools):" OpenStreetMap (via QuickOSM) "

  Spatial Analysis Workflow
1. Infrastructure Extraction:" Mapped schools and hospitals to evaluate population and critical service density across districts. "
2. Drainage Buffer Creation:" Applied a 200-meter effective service area buffer around the existing waterway and drainage network."
3. Vulnerability Scoring:" Calculated the concentration of critical facilities falling outside the drainage buffer to generate a vulnerability heat map. "

 Imporant Notes
Vulnerable Hotspots:" The northern districts (dark red areas) contain a high density of critical facilities but severely lack drainage network coverage. These areas are highly vulnerable to urban flooding during extreme rainfall events and should be prioritized for infrastructure expansion."
Covered Zones:" The central and southern areas (orange/yellow areas) show better integration between critical facilities and existing waterways. "
